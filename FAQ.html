FAQ: CrashScan & EDR Reports

Is my vehicle supported?

Vehicle support varies by vehicle make and region.  Please check the list for the most up to date support: https://collisionsciences.ca/reports/check_support/

The CrashScan app is the most universal EDR tool available worldwide; however, there are a few vehicles makes that are not supported, although these vehicles may have EDRs and can have data retrieved through other methods, these include:
- 1996-1999 GM vehicles (using non-standard OBD-1)
- Subaru (use local expert)
- Jaguar/Land Rover (use manufacturer)

Can crash data be erased over time?

Passenger vehicle Event Data Recorders (EDRs) can store between 2 and 6 crash events, for all severities of collision, such as for "airbag deployment events" and also for minor "non-deployment crash events".  

These crash events DO NOT automatically erase, with one exeption: 2011 and older General Motors (GM) brand vehicles that experience minor non-deployment events.  

Advice 
Determine the vehicle usage, estimate its ignition cycles would be much less than 250 times since the accident.

(North American Low-Velocity Impact Investigations): 
This data clearing factor would make a difference if it's an LVI report and proof of a non-event is needed.  The Collision Sciences EDR report has a disclaimer note.
